W3C Position Paper - Linked Data needs a Data Location Service

Issue:

It’s hard to find SPARQL endpoints on the internet, it’s even harder to find out what relevant data such endpoints might have.

Also their is a discrepancy between the (uri) domain of a SPARQL endpoint and the domain of some of the subject-uri’s of datasets behind such an endpoint.

Why?

The discrepancy between the domain of a SPARQL endpoint providing linked data and instance subject-uri’s cannot be resolved.

How come, the discrepancy cannot be resolved?:

Behind every internet domain, is some kind of organisation.

Each such organisation has customers or users and most likely suppliers as well.

Customers and Suppliers are by nature foreign entities to these domains.

Meaning if you want to take note of services or products sold to customers or provided by suppliers, using Linked Data, you will do so with uri’s foreign to your domain.

In this way the discrepancy will always be there.

From another perspective:

One of the powers of Linked Data is the possibility to relate data, you wouldn't think of upfront. This requires a source which doesn't have a pre-defined meaning about how data should be related. Just a bunch of dataset descriptions (meta data), that allow the user to decide what data is needed for their solution.

Suggested Solution:

Provide a central Linked Data Locations service similar to the service provided by dns between domain names and ip addresses. The service could largely make use of the VoID vocabulary and have a triplestore with SPARQL endpoint on a well known (purl) address.

Additional benefits:

A central service, part of the low-level operations could allows datasets to:

  • add attributes about the quality of a dataset
  • Show a list of well known datasets
